Which visual approach feels right?

"macOS look and feel" can mean a few different things

A

Window-in-Window

The page is framed as a macOS desktop — a menu bar at top, then the app popup screenshot "floats" on a desktop wallpaper background, like you're looking at someone's screen showing the app.

B

Menu Bar Framing

The actual macOS menu bar strip sits at the top of the page. Below it is a clean, modern landing page. The app icon appears in the menu bar like it would on a real Mac. More of a "brand accent" than a full simulation.

C

Apple Design Language

No simulated menu bar — instead, borrow Apple's design DNA: San Francisco font, lots of whitespace, subtle glassmorphism, rounded corners, minimal color. Feels like an Apple product page.
